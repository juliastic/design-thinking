Design Thinking - Understanding Design Processes

As part of the course Design Thinking: Ideation we have been tasked in groups of four to develop a tangible product which improves daily life of inhabitants of Vienna's Seestadt .

A lot of different aspects have to be considered when designing a new prototype.

We have focused on finding a solution to the increasing number of cars to the Seestadt.

During the course, we had to work on several topics to cycle through the design progress. The tasks ranged from designing a provocative requisites to conducting a design workshop.

Design Workshop - Results

We have agreed on two concrete tangible ideas to cover next semester: a CO 2 display in Seestadt & bike sharing service.

Design Thinking - Conclusion

Understanding the needs and requirements of users is a complex task. Even more so is designing a system which is actually wanted by users. We will most likely follow up on the idea of displaying CO 2 pollution in Seestadt & adapting parking prices based on the measured values.

In the follow up course Design Thinking: Implementation we will further refine our idea to create a prototype.
